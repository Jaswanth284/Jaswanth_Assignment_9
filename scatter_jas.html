      <h2>Scatter Plot: Renewable Energy vs CO₂ Emissions</h2>

      <img src="images/jas_scatter.png" alt="Scatter plot Renewable Energy vs CO2">

      <p>

        This scatter plot shows the relationship between renewable energy

        percentage and CO₂ emissions per capita. The x-axis represents the

        share of renewable energy in the total energy mix, while the y-axis

        shows CO₂ emissions in tons per person. Colors encode the continent

        of each country and the size of the points encodes power consumption

        per person. Larger points represent countries that use more

        electricity. This visualization helps check whether higher renewable

        energy usage is associated with lower emissions while also revealing

        differences in electricity demand.

      </p>

    </section>


    <section>

      <h2>Optimized Scatter Plot (2024, Log CO₂ Scale)</h2>

      <img src="images/jas_scatter_opt.png" alt="Optimized scatter plot for 2024">

      <p>

        The optimized scatter plot focuses on data from the year 2024 and

        uses a logarithmic scale for CO₂ emissions. Filtering to a single

        year removes visual clutter from multiple years and highlights the

        most recent situation for each country. The log scale spreads out

        emission values that vary widely, so small and large emission levels

        are easier to compare. This version makes patterns more visible and

        improves readability while still encoding continent and forest area

        through color and size.

      </p>
